Consumer expectations have increased, with the market demanding better products with more functionality, more quickly. In addition to the consumer market changes, the shrinking cost of silicon has practically eliminated the cost barriers that previously existed with 32-bit microprocessor technology. In response to these changes, the complexity, diversity, and sophistication of application development has increased considerably. The success of a product often depends on getting it to market ahead of all the other competition. Add to this truncated time-to-market cycle the fact that these next-generation applications are extremely complex and require a high level of expertise in embedded systems development.
