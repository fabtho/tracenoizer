. The California Census Research Data Center The California Census Research Data Center The California Census Research Data Center (CCRDC) is a newly established partnership between the U. Bureau of the Census and the University of California. Under this partnership the Census Bureau provides access to non-public microdata from the its economic (business) and demographic (household) data programs for research conducted in the CCRDC's laboratories at UCLA and UC Berkeley. A Research Data Centre is a combination of a secure Statistics Canada office on campus and a statistical analysis computing laboratory. As a statistical analysis computing laboratory, a Research Data Centre has a closed local area network consisting of a powerful server supporting a mix of PCs and Macintosh workstations with a range of statistical analysis software. For a more complete description of this computing facility at the University of Alberta, follow the link to Services. The proposal for Research Data Centres arose from the report of a joint-committee of the SSHRC and Statistics Canada in January 1999. Known as the Canadian Initiative for Social Statistics, this report contains several proposals to improve the state of quantitative research and policy analysis in Canada.
