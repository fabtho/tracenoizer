Abstract Automation of business processes based on web services requires a notation for the specification of message exchange behavior among participating web services. XLANG is expected to serve as the basis for automated protocol engines that can track the state of process instances and help enforce protocol correctness in message flows. Document Status This is an initial public draft release of the XLANG specification that focuses on the language constructs used to define the behavior of an individual web service that participates in business processes. We also address the approach to combining such services into multi-party business processes. This release does not provide detailed examples and tutorial exposition.
