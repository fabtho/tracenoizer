An Investigation of Heat Dissipation In Ultra-Dense Computer Architectures An Investigation of Heat Dissipation In Ultra-Dense Computer Architectures David Marcus Nanosystems Group The MITRE Corporation McLean, VA 22102 November 1997 Contents Abstract Introduction Background Method Heat Dissipation Discussion Conclusion Appendix Acknowledgments Bibliography Abstract This paper presents and applies a method for exploring issues pertinent to the design of ultra-dense computer architectures. The particular issue to be explored here is the heat dissipation of a quantum-dot cellular automata (QCA) computer architecture. QCA is a novel approach to ultra-dense computer architectures proposed by Craig Lent and Wolfgang Porod of University of Notre Dame.
