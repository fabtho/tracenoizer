Low Temperature Heat Distribution Systems combined with Domestic Heat Pumps Low Temperature Heat Distribution Systems combined with Domestic Heat Pumps Dimitra Sakellari and Per Lundqvist Introduction Considering the operation of heat pumps, it is generally known that it is beneficial to have a low temperature on the condenser side, because the coefficient of performance (COP) is improved and the efficiency is increased. Hence, it is an advantage to have a low-temperature heat sink. As far as domestic heat pumps are concerned it is better to use a low-temperature, heat-distribution system.
