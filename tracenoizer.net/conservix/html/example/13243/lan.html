LANs are designed to be user friendly and there is a lot of flexibility built into their operating systems. Unfortunately, this often leads to situations where LANs are set up with little centralized control or planning. This often results in very loose or in some instances no consideration for the security of the LAN. LAN administrators are normally given a wide choice of options in setting up the security of the LAN and without some careful planning, the administrator may inadvertently leave the LAN and its users wide open to attacks from unscrupulous users. Third party access control and security software is also available for most LAN environments.
