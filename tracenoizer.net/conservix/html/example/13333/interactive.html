The audio-visual and network modules of the eMUSE architecture support scenarios networked in real time and based on Java and the VRML browser. As well as including several persons in a communal real space and depicting this in a virtual environment, eMUSE also allows the participation of spatially distant users via the Internet. The platform provides important components of the performance arts and links them with interactive and networked scenarios - the possibility of free movement in space, the transference of that movement in real time into a virtual environment, the participation of several performers and their interaction with each other. This investigated the practicability of the interface in a public space with a high fluctuation and a heterogeneous user group.
