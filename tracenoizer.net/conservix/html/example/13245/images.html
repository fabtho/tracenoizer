Opening with a hospitalized woman proudly declaring her commitment to the resistance and asserting "We don't want a dictator," the film closes with images of Ceausescu's corpse taken after his execution less than two weeks later. Rather than making a straightforward documentary, Farocki and codirector Andrej Ujica have opted for a reflexive approach that pays as much attention to formal concerns as to the political changes themselves. Watching these films consecutively reveals that Farocki can't match the consistency of Marker or Resnais, but even if his experiments sometimes fail, Images of the World makes an excellent case for his inquisitive brand of filmmaking.
