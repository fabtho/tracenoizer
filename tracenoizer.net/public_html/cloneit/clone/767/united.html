Institute for Research on Women and Gender The Institute for Research on Women and Gender provides stimulation, coordination and support for research on women and gender at the University of Michigan. This research explores differences and commonalities among and between women and men in the multicultural United States and internationally. The Institute coordinates existing research activities by bringing together faculty who have related interests from different units of the University. Walker organized 3,000 agents to sell her hair treatment door-to-door and in 1910 transferred her business--by then the Madam CJ Walker Manufacturing Company--to Indianapolis, Indiana. Her "Walker agents" --saleswomen dressed in long black skirts and white blouses -- became familiar figures in the black communities of the United States and the Caribbean. Walker was president and sole proprietor of her company, and she soon became one of the best known figures in America. The "Walker System" coiffure became popular in Europe through the example of noted entertainer, Josephine Baker.
