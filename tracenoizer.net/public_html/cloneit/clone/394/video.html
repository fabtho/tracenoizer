The word "demo" does not refer to a demo of a game; it refers to the demonstration of programming, artistic, and musical talent working together synchronously. Demos first popularly arose in the late 80's and early 90's on the Amiga and PC platform. Demo teams viewed Amigas, PCs, and even video game consoles like the Atari 2600 as a means to create art. Demos have arguably boosted artistic technology in interactive multimedia and video games to the state they are at today. For example, some of the first real-time 3D engines for the PC were created for demos.
