Bach He studied with his father and his relative Johann Elias (39), and on his father's death he took a post at the court of Count Wilhelm of Schaumburg-Lippe at Bueckeburg, where he remained throughout his career, becoming Concert-Meister in 1759. He composed in most of the genres of the time; his chamber and keyboard music is lightweight, but his keyboard concertos and symphonies show Viennese Classical influence and some of the dramatic cantatas and oratorios (some to texts by Herder, for a time his colleague at the progressive Bueckeburg court) show imaginative expression. ..
