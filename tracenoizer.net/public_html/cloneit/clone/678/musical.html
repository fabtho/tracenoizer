He toured extensively throughout Europe, visiting Britain in 1838 and 1849. He married in 1825 and was the father of Johann II (1825-1899), Josef (1827-1870) and Eduard I (1835-1916). In the 55 years of his musical career, his output was prolific - he wrote over 550 works (his opus listed works totalling 479). His best known operettas are Die Fledermaus (The Bat) (1874), A Night in Venice (1883) and The Gypsy Baron (1885). He came to London in 1867 where he gave a series of 63 concerts at Covent Garden Opera House.
