Rather than a juxtaposition of analytic concepts to their body-work, they have sought links between verbalisation, image productions and energetical movements in the body. In an other way they considered the relational aspect in therapeutic cure more carefully and have introduced the Freudian notions of transference and counter-transference in their work. The Psycho-Organic Circle and the Primary Impulse Training offer a reading and diagnosis scale allowing to understand how primary energy, first undifferenciated finds an incarnate shape in a specific action or expression. On the other way the Primary Impulse Training shows the strength of images as levers of transformation in a therapeutic process. A chain of daydreamings creating precise archetypal images, enables to symbolise the emergences of the unconscious and undertightens the individuation of the person.
We also have been asked to advice for the establishment of new trainings for traditional healers. The increase in pluriformity of treatment modalities offered in a clinic contributes to the effectiveness of the care and the emancipation of herbal medicine. 1 Training By providing training the knowledge becomes institutionalized, and does not remain a foreign body, which undergoes the fate of rejection as so many developmental projects. Since 1993 several dozens of doctors, midwives, nurses and pharmacists have been trained in Ghana by the Dutch branch of Homeopaths without Borders. 2 Offical recognition This policy is supported by the Ghanaian Ministry of Health, to which Homeopaths without Borders has given a consultancy for a legal framework for complementary medicine (registration of practitioners, code of ethics, policies for education drugs and research).
