Ash Genaidy, associate professor of industrial engineering, discovered a way to bring economics into the engineering curriculum early on, and the results will benefit the mentally handicapped throughout Northern Kentucky. Genaidy realized that co-op experience and a senior design project provide a great deal of hands-on experience for young engineers. What they don't always learn is how to evaluate the cost and benefits and choose the best alternative based on economic grounds. So, he designed a new course, "Engineering Economy" to give juniors more experience in cost-effective design.
