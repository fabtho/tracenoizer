. In July 1959, the Jewish writer Paul Celan travelled with his wife and child to Sils-Maria on vacation. Adorno's famous comment, to write poetry after Ausschwitz is barbaric, had caused a controversy that still reverberates today. But Adorno didn't show up and the meeting never took place. Celan broke off his holiday and returned to Paris where he wrote the impressive piece of poetic prose, 'Conversation In The Mountains'.
