Includes 24 page insert designed and produced in Zurich, Switzerland. SOLD OUT (1990) "Most of the work that people do in Basel is meant as exercises and are isolated processes from which you can learn. And you're supposed to use that knowledge in varied ways. But too many designers who studied at Basel did not really understand what it was about and ended up producing school exercises in their professional work. " - Hamish Muir, 8vo (1990) "To teach is not difficult.
Designers should have more respect for their readers. A typographer should be the most sensitive reader because his task is to prepare the work for public criticism. There are an endless number of possible ways of looking at it. The only thing I can do as designer is to animate the person through my message. He himself should act, should analyze, and reproduce the visual message for himself.
