Damien Cheers' Mercedes Mercedes-Benz SLK February 1997. The Mercedes-Benz SLK was given one of the most important design awards in the world. It was chosen by the renowned "Industrie Forum Design Hannover," from 1,300 products, as the only car in the "Top Ten" "iF-Product Design Award 1997. The peppy Roadster with the vario roof that opens up in seconds was the only car out of 700 entries from 28 countries to be picked out. The entries were judged on the degree of innovation including workmanship, materials, ergonomics, safety and life-span as well as on shape and functionality.

Italian design had little real influence on German design until Memphis appeared on the scene in the early 1980s. Ettore Sottsass was the best loved of all with his coloured plastic laminate panels for Abet Laminati, among the most popular creations of the Memphis era, whose "violently" decorated surfaces graced many a German furnishing product. However, young German designers quickly opposed the colourful Italian trend with their own austerely unexuberant plain MDF and untreated metal furnishings. Today both Germany and Italy offer a range of different design trends, as the understated elegance of new modesty and the back-to-nature philosphy of ecological design develop in parallel with bright colours, pale wood and new man-made materials.
