The Dalai Lama is believed to be an incarnation of Bodhisattva Avalokiteshvara; enlightened beings who have delayed their own spiritual union with the supreme spirit in order to return and serve humanity. Tenzin Gyatso, born in 1935, is the 14th and current Dalai Lama. As the spiritual and political leader of Tibet, he tried to cooperate with China after its army invaded Tibet in 1950, but brutal acts of repression forced him to flee in 1959. He has lived in exile in Dharamsala, India since, and travels the world seeking support for the Tibetan cause. Since China's pogrom began, over 6,000 monasteries have been destroyed and one million Tibetans killed.
