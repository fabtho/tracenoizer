This river town maintains its heritage by standing guard over its Colonial, Georgian, Federal, Greek Revival and Victorian architectural styles. Its citizens still maintain an attitude of friendliness and Southern gentility. It was settled in 1710 by Swiss and German immigrants who named it after the Swiss capital of Bern. The town was officially founded by Swiss Baron Christoph deGraffenried. Just like any Swiss city, New Bern is distinguished by its red-brick clock tower above City Hall.
