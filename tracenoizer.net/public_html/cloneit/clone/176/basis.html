This fund contains more money (16 million guilders each year) for 1997-2001 than what the Ministry of Culture provides on its own for international activities(16) . The ambitions of this fund mirror the lack of a clear vision. Its basis is primarily rooted in mainly foreign policy priorities - evident in the choice of countries, stressing the importance of large-scale thematic and regional projects and the lack of cultural-policy choices (Raad voor Cultuur 1998, 16). Also this fund actually contradicts the arm's-length-principle of cultural policy in general; normally it is the independent cultural funding bodies who decide on support but with this fund a commission of the two ministries decides. The arts world and the Council for Culture have complained that criteria for this fund are unclear.
The object is to take measures in different fields, at the same time and in a coherent way. The communities are supposed to elaborate a policy plan for a period of 3 years, in dialogue with the inhabitants. This policy plan is the basis for a covenant with the Flemish government. In 1996 the budget was 4,4 billion BEF (1,1 billion EURO). There is an annual increase of the budget with 1 billion BEF (247,9 million EURO).
