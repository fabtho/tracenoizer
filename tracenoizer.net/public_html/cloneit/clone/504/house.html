A treat for fans and the best possible introduction for the uninitiated. . Much of the Marx Brothers' humour always came from their bare-faced lack of respect for authority and their subversion of the codes of social behaviour, so what better place to turn them loose than the opera house. What place is more filled with pompous old windbags and prima donnas just begging for the Groucho treatment. Around a skeleton plot involving the brothers' attempts to give a pair of Italian opera singers a big break in America, director Wood and screenwriters Kaufman and Ryskind (whose job was mainly to just contain the anarchy in the vague framework of a story) create a masterpiece of dizzying levels.

University Film Society University Film Society provides campus with top cinema The University Film Society (UFS) is a non-profit film exhibitor right on the U of M campus. Called the epicenter of local film culture by the Minnesota Daily, the UFS runs films at Bell Auditorium and Nicholson Auditorium 360 days a year. Although it brings in top-notch art house films and the occasional revival show, foreign films are its real specialty. The UFS has been a part of campus life since 1962, when it was founded as Minnesota's first (and only) non-profit organization to exhibit foreign and independent films. Last year it was called the "Best Independent Movie Theatre" by City Pages .
