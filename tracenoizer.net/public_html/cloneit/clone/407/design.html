Industrial Design is concerned with all the human aspects of machine-made products and their relationship to people and the environment. The designer is responsible for these products and their impact on society and nature. Industrial design deals with consumer products as well as industrial products.
We are continually bombarded with so many visual sensations that we cannot pay conscious attention to all of them. Instead we learn through experience with our environment how sensations can be interpreted and which ones can be safely ignored. Most people already know a great deal about the art of design, but few of us recognize our own visual sophistication. To design successfully, we must create and manipulate visual information so that it will have the meaning intended. The design program offers both two-dimensional and three-dimensional experiences.

The Design Review Board reviews projects from the perspective of the larger context in which the project is located rather than in terms of program and user needs. The Design Review Board's review responsibility is the "civic" mission of a project, not its "private", or functional one. .

The Green Design Initiative at Carnegie Mellon University Carnegie Mellon University began a campus-wide Green Design Initiative in 1992 to promote environmentally conscious engineering, product and process design, manufacturing, and architecture. The initiative involves forming partnerships with industrial corporations, foundations, and government agencies to develop joint research and education programs which improve environmental quality while encouraging sustainable economic development.
