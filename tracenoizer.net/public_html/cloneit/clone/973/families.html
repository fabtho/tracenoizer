Family Centered Services, including Family Preservation services, are offered to a family when risk of the permanency and well-being of the family unit first becomes apparent. Their purpose is to provide support and intervention to the family unit before placement of the children becomes imminent. Some families who receive these prevention services are not known to the Department, but are served by community providers. Others have become known to the Department through abuse or neglect reports. After investigation, whether a report is determined to be founded or unfounded, if serious family needs are discovered which may ultimately lead to placement of the children in foster care, intervention is offered.
