Design Jury and Architect-Engineer Evaluation Board Design Jury and Architect-Engineer Evaluation Board DESIGN JURY A 10-member independent design jury, the majority of whom are nationally recognized design professionals, evaluated design concepts for the National World War II Memorial. The jury judged design proposals of six competition finalists selected from approximately 400 initial entries, and made recommendations to the Architect-Engineer Evaluation Board. Hugh Hardy, FAIA, partner in Hardy Holzman Pfeiffer Associates.
Entries were evaluated on their originality, appropriateness, feasibility, and compliance with project requirements. The board selected six design visions to compete in Stage II. Stage II design concepts were judged by a Design Jury. The Design Jury identified the strengths and weaknesses of each concept and provided a rationale for their final ranking to the A-E Board. Bill Lacy, president of Purchase College, State University of New York, was the professional advisor for the competition.
Each team will have worked on the project for a number of weeks.
