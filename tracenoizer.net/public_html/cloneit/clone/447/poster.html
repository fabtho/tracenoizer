Agnieszka Maria Wasieczko Back to the first page of Buzz Section. . 24 (607) Poster Art Graphic Artists Strut Their Stuff The International Poster Biennial in Warsaw is the world's first and foremost serial event of its kind. The Warsaw competition, organized under the patronage of the Council of the Graphic Design Association (Icograda), has won recognition from artists and critics since its inception. The world's most famous graphic artists have been among the competition's winners.
In 1990, the city decided to become an international center for posters and started the yearly Rencontres Internationales des Arts Graphiques under the expert guidance of Alain Weill. Most exhibitions remain open until July 26, with the exception of Ralph Schraivogel's one man show and the exhibition of turn of the century posters, which closed on June 28. The prize winners The theme of this years poster competition was Photography, and about 5000 invitations to submit recent works were sent to poster designers in forty countries. 1350 posters were received and prescreened down to 84 by a french jury, and these are exhibited and shown in the catalogue. .
