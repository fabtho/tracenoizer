The salt of good grade was abundant, but the costs of production and delivery made continuance economically unwise. With the closing of the salt works, the Grahams opened a land office for the sale of lots and acreage and later leasing, for even so early the thought of oil production was gaining headway. Colonel Graham also mined for gold on the north side of Twin Mountain, but the vein he found did not warrant production. A combination grist and saw mill and cotton gin was located on Salt Creek in a three-story structure, which proved an excellent and necessary business.
