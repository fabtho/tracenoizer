" (Hazel Rosenstrauch) Was passiert, wenn gruener Tee auf Milch trifft. Man folge dem jungen oesterreichischen Kuenstler, der in New York eine Japanerin kennenlernt, ihr nach Tokio folgt und dort eine Amerikanerin aus dem Chelsea-Hotel wiedertrifft. Ein sehr unoesterreichischer Film des jungen Linzer Kosmopoliten Edgar Honetschlaeger, der sich bisher vor allem als bildender Kuenstler und Performance-Aktivist einen Namen gemacht hat. Ein vieldiskutierter Beitrag bei der Viennale und der einzige oesterreichische Spielfilm in einer offiziellen Sektion der Berlinale 1998. .
A privileged young Japanese woman finds freedom in the role of an elevator girl, where her many choices are reduced to 'up' and 'down'. A radio announcer from New York falls in love with a rogue salaryman. ''Edgar Honetschlaeger might well have made something like a cult classic. By which I mean a film far from mainstream which at the same time uses mainstream methods but has encoded it into something sufficiently dissident to attract but is not anarchic enough to repel. Milk is a 'Maerchen' about falling out rather than (fairy tale pattern) coming together.
A radio English teacher from New York who falls in love with a rogue salaryman. Spirits live, cultures clash and twains meet in this hip, atmospheric, and truly original film. Milk is the first feature by Austrian artist Edgar Honetschlaeger. 1989 he moved to NYC and later to Tokyo which he has called his primary home since 1992. Based in Tokyo, California, New York and Vienna long before the global villager became a fashion, Honetschlaeger dedicates Milk to total borderlessness in a manifest of unencumbered lightness.
