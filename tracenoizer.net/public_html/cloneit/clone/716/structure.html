Saturating mutagenesis studies of a highly conserved Asp564(LHR) in the junction of the 3rd intracellular (i3) loop and transmembrane domain 6 (TM6) suggested a participation of this anionic residue in a salt bridge stabilizing the inactive receptor conformation. However, substitution of all conserved cationic residues at the LHR and TSHR cytoplasmic surface did not support this hypothesis for glycoprotein receptors. Adapting the identified N-cap conformation to the LHR structure model assures not only the extension of helix-TM6 into the cytoplasm but has also the consequence, that the backbone conformation for the two residues preceding the Asp564 N-cap-conformation is oriented tightly towards TM3. Subsequently the glycoprotein receptors are sealed between TM6 and TM3 at the intracellular site. Thus the interior core of the receptor is prevented for infiltration of G-protein sequences between TM3 and TM6.
