Becoming a close friend to someone was dangerous, however. Hayley and most others who lived there were not orphans but came from troubled families who financially or emotionally couldn't raise their children. Hayley's mother sent him there along with his brother and sister when he was 4. Hayley said the Children's Home gave him a strong work ethic and discipline that he wouldn't have gotten if he stayed at home with a mother who was struggling to make ends meet. Hayley was approached by the overseer of the home, who told him his mother was able to care for him again and he would be going home that day.
