Two nearby examples of super star clusters (in NGC 1569 and NGC 1705) have dynamical masses, mass densities, and predicted mass-to-light ratios that are virtually indistinguishable from those of evolved globular clusters in the Galaxy. This provides compelling evidence that at least some super star clusters truly are present-day analogs of young globular clusters.
