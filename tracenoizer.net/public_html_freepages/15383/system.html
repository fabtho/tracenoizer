In my view, the Kinetic is a gimmick, both technologically and from a marketing point of view. It is basically a quartz watch, whose timekeeping mechanism is identical to any present-day quartz watch. Its only innovation is the fact that it replaces the aspirin-sized (or smaller) battery with a much more bulky generating system and a very high grade capacitor for energy storage. The capacitor does hold a good bit of charge when fully charged (I think I heard 100 hours' worth of running time) but if you go away for a few days and leave the watch in a drawer, it is pretty sure it will no longer be running when you return. I suspect this is not a 60Hz circuit but something like a tape head demagnetizer that inductively charges the capacitor through a built-in rectifier circuit, by induction, the way some electric toothbrushes get their batteries charged without actual contact.
