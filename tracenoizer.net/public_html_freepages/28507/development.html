. Largaespada's laboratory is working to exploit insertional mutagenesis for cancer gene discovery. A mouse model of murine leukemia virus induced acute myeloid leukemia is being used to identify and characterize genes that have a role in myeloid leukemia development. Largaespada is focusing on genes that have a role in regulating Ras superfamily signaling, including the Neurofibromatosis type 1 gene and a new family of calcium and diacylglycerol regulated guanine nucleotide exchange factor genes. In addition, they are adapting a recently constructed transposon system for insertional mutagenesis in mouse somatic and germline cells.
