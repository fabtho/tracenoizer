2100 DeWayne Wells, Executive Director Brother Vincent Reyes O. Brother Vincent Reyes is the Pastoral Director of the Capuchin Soup Kitchen of Detroit, but he's also a photojournalist who has enjoyed capturing the stories of people's lives on film for more than thirty-five years. As a Brother in the Order of Friars Minor Capuchin since 1961, Brother Vincent has served in local Parishes, worked in Migrant Ministry, and spent twelve years as a Retreat Director. After assignments in South and Central America, he returned to the United States to be a Vocation Director for the Capuchins. Prior to his duties at the Soup Kitchen, Brother Vincent for eleven years was the Chaplain in a major trauma center in the Chicago area, spending almost all of his time with families in the emergency room as they faced unexpected tragedy and sorrow in their lives.
He noted though that there were troubles in the NSW and WA chapters, but that the Victorian Chapter was performing extremely well. The Chapter President was not accessible, due to loosing his job with the University in which he worked. There had been some minor activities but most of the active local membership, had involvements on the Federal scene, and found it difficult to translate this into local activities. New South Wales - Had been moving along slowly, but had run into a spot of difficulty with the sudden resignation of the Chapter President and Treasurer. There were plans to hold an Annual General Meeting in the immediate future and to get things back to where they had been prior to the beginning of the year.
