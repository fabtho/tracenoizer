The objective of my PhD thesis project is to study patterns in resistance to different plant enemies, using Rapid-cyling Brassica rapa (Brassicaceae) as a model system. My work explores the influences of atmospheric nitrogen deposition (anthropogenically-derived fixed nitrogen compounds which are deposited onto plant and soil surfaces) on plant physiology, growth, and reproduction. I am exploring both the direct effects of N deposition on plants (that is, those effects due to increased availability of fixed nitrogen) and more indirect effects of N deposition (deposition-induced changes in interactions between plants and their insect herbivores). I am exploring these interactions through experimental work in the SUNYSB greenhouse, field experiments at Brookhaven National Laboratory on eastern Long Island, and computer modeling work. I am interested in aggressive competition while foraging in non-human primates.
