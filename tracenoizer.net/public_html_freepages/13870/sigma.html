History The first Pi Sigma Alpha chapter was founded in 1920 at the University of Texas to bring students and faculty together in the study of government and politics. The Alpha Zeta chapter of Pi Sigma Alpha was founded in 1938 at the University of Maryland, College Park. Membership Membership in Pi Sigma Alpha is by invitation only and is based solely upon academic merit.

Sigma Alpha is a national collegiate organization designed to promote women in agriculture and strengthen the bonds of friendship between them. The sorority was founded at the Ohio State University in 1978, and has since grown to include chapters across the United States. Each sister of Sigma Alpha learns to explore her professional career opportunities, serve her community, participate in leadership activities and foster excellence on a personal and interpersonal level.
