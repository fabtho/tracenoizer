Co-Director, Advanced Visualization Technology Center (AVTC). Faculty Member, Computational Engineering and Science (CES) Program. Professor Johnson's research interests are in the area of scientific computing. Professor Johnson was awarded a Young Investigator's (FIRST) Award from the NIH in 1992, the NSF National Young Investigator (NYI) Award in 1994, and the NSF Presidential Faculty Fellow (PFF) award in 1995. In 1996 he received a DOE Computational Science Award and in 1997 recevied the Par Excellence Award from the University of Utah Alumni Association and the Presidential Teaching Scholar Award.
Computer Science and Mathematical Sciences (1982), M. Johnson's research interests are primarily in the area of wireless and mobile networking protocols. Prior to joining Rice's Department of Computer Science in Fall 2000, Johnson was an Associate Professor of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University, where he was a faculty member from 1992-2000. Johnson is leading the joint Monarch Project between Rice and Carnegie Mellon, developing adaptive networking protocols and protocol interfaces to allow truly seamless wireless and mobile host networking. The goal of this work is to enable mobile hosts to communicate with each other and with stationary or wired hosts, transparently making the most efficient use of the best network connectivity available to the mobile host at any time.
