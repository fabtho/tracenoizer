In Refuge she led readers through the pain of her family's losses using the beauty of the surrounding desert, its birds and their habitat in the Great Salt Lake as a parallel to her family's trials and tribulations in the Great Basin. "I always begin and end with birds," said the naturalist-in-residence of the Utah Museum of Natural History. In "Refuge" she traces the effects of flooding around the Great Salt Lake on the bird population and shares her agony as the women in her family succumb to breast cancer - the effect of living downwind of nuclear bomb testing.
