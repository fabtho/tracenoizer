Community Technology Fund's objectives are to invest directly with other venture capital co-investors in entrepreneurial companies; to assist in business formation and licensing technologies developed at Boston University; and to advise the University on its investments in Venture Funds as a Limited Partner. . Boston University, through CTF, was one of the first universities to develop a focused venture capital and technology transfer program. CTF began investing in 1975 and has held equity positions in over 125 companies in the United States, through direct investments in venture deals and as a limited partner in Venture Funds. CTF also manages the Technology Transfer Program at Boston University and assists the University's faculty in identifying, protecting, and commercializing the University's intellectual property.

The University Community Plan will be incorporated into the Merced County General Plan, and will establish the patterns and guidelines for future development of the University Community. In developing the Plan, Merced County is closely collaborating with the UC Merced, landowners, and the local community to address a wide range of issues of common concern. As a result of an extensive analysis of a range of alternative sites in eastern Merced County for location of the UC Merced campus and the associated University Community, UC and the County have jointly endorsed a shift of the location of the UC Merced campus to the southwest corner of the Virginia Smith Trust lands, on and around the Merced Hills Golf Course.
