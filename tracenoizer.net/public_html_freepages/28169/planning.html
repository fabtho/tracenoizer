As is always the case, a small or cosmetic physical change or minor job alteration may provide a false sense of improvement, causing more harm than good. Back to Top Work Practices Workplace and job improvements may be derived from the modification of work practices such as increasing employee fitness for work through education and training and tackling extremes of ergonomic stress through job rotation or utilizing rest breaks. Such methods require strong management involvement in planning, implementing, and monitoring; i. , the improvement is achieved through administrative control. The success of such measures in eliminating or controlling ergonomic problems is limited; and, in time, the solution is very likely to turn into a set of procedures or rules ignored by many and remembered by only a few, mainly at safety meetings and in the course of litigation.
