Roberts and Thomas Merrill connected two computers with a low speed dial-up telephone line creating the first (however small) wide-area computer network ever built. 1969 By the end of 1969, four host computers were connected together into the initial ARPANET and the growth of the Internet had commenced. Computers were added quickly to the ARPANET during the following years, and work proceeded on completing a functionally complete Host-to-Host protocol and other network software. In December 1970 the Network Working Group (NWG) finished the initial ARPANET Host-to-Host protocol, called the Network Control Protocol (NCP). As the ARPANET sites completed implementing NCP during the period 1971-1972, network users finally could begin to develop applications.
