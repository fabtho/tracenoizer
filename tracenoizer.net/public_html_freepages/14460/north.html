In this case the Dakota choose to battle for their land and freedom at the same time African-Americans are about to gain emancipation. After being swindled into debt by fur traders via purchasing on credit due to late rations, the Dakota Indians were practically forced into signing a treaty relinquishing all lands west of the Mississippi. Mix informed the Dakota chiefs that the government also required all lands north of the Minnesota River which included half of their reservation. The treaty, though it seemed unfair to the Dakota and was in fact unfair, still provided a lofty image of "what a great deal" the Indians were receiving. With the declaration and ratification of this treaty, the demand for land by white settlers rose tremendously.
