In the Symbolic Simulation Technique, sometimes called the Wizard of Oz Technique, a human expert acts like an expert system, intercepting help messages from the user and providing advice in the problem-solving task. In Storyboarding, the ultimate system is portrayed by a series of nonfunctional display screens that depict a typical sequence of interactions between user and system. Some critique-type KATs present, as a stimulus for criticism, parts of the conceptual structure of the decision process being modeled by the expert system. Thus, for example, the Teachback Technique has the KE telling the SME how the process works, the SME pointing out remaining flaws in the KE's conceptualization.
