Specifically, the IRRR serves the interests of the Taconite Tax Relief Area (TTRA), a geographical region encompassing approximately 13,000 square miles. IRRR programs, including those that encourage economic development and tourism, are funded by taxes levied against the region's taconite mining companies. The agency and its programs receive no money from the state's general fund. In addition to assisting existing businesses and communities, as well as providing incentives for business relocation and enhancement of tourism to the region, the agency owns and operates Ironworld Discovery Center in Chisholm and Giants Ridge Golf and Ski Resort near Biwabik. History The IRRR was created by the 1941 Minnesota Legislature to help ease northeastern Minnesota's dependence on the natural ore and timber industries.
