She first appeared in Japan with a Galaconcert with Tokyo Philharmonic Orchestra in Tokyo. With great success she appeared as Penelope in the creation of Leinen aus Smyrna by Edward Rushton at the Opera Festival Munich. She was a prize winner at the International Belvedere Competition for Operasingers in Vienna. She also debued as Fata Morgana in Prokofjev's "The Love to Three Oranges" with Janos Kulka at the Stuttgart State Opera and sang the female lead in Saint-Saens opera "Samson et Dalila" with the NDR Hannover with Eiji Oue. Miss Pinter gave her New York Debut at Carnegie Hall (Weill Recital Hall) with a solorecital featuring works by Wagner and Mahler.

Brigitte Pinter gab ihr Operndebuet in Schuberts Fierrabras unter Claudio Abbado bei den Wiener Festwochen. Abbados persoenliche Empfehlung ermoeglichte ihr das Gesangsstudium an der Juilliard School of Music in New York bei Alan Seale, wofuer ihr ein Fulbrightstipendium (USA) und ein Foerderungsstipendium der Musikhochschule Wien zuerkannt wurde. Abbados personal recommendation enabled her studies at the renowned Juilliard School of Music in New York (Voice with Alan Seale). She was granted a Fulbright Scholarship from the United States and a promotional Scholarship from the Vienna Academy of Music. She was a prize winner at the International Belvedere Competition for Operasingers in Vienna. Shortly after the competition the young singer gave her debut as Sister Jeanne in Krzysztof Pendereckis opera The Devils Of Loudun in Vienna higly acclaimed by press and audience. Miss Pinter gave her New York Debut at Carnegie Hall with a solorecital featuring works by Wagner and Mahler.
