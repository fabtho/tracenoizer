Abstract The University of Massachusetts Ascender system relies on a single reconstruction strategy that is tuned to extract only one class of buildings by detecting single-level, flat, rectangular rooftops. The present paper describes initial work on a more general 3D reconstruction system that deos not rely on a single type of data, and is not restricted to a small predefined class of buildings. The approach involves multiple alternative detection and reconstruction strategies, invoked by clear contextual cues, that combine a wider set of algorithms and features for generating and fusing 3D and 3D information.
