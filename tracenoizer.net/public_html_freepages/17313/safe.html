This program is believed to be the most effective for reducing crime rates in small neighborhoods, said Annabelle Johnson, a member of CUFA for 17 years. The program allows residents from neighborhoods such as Elmwood and East Walnut Hills to anonymously report crimes that they see taking place. "This has allowed people who are afraid to get involved to keep themselves safe while they keep their neighborhood safe," Johnson said. Anonymously reporting crimes has led to the arrest of some "big fish" drug dealers that threaten the livelihood of neighborhoods, she said. Members of CUFA meet quarterly with the Cincinnati Police Division to receive updates on how programs are working, she said.
