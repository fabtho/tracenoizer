McGovern Professor of Information Systems at the MIT Sloan School of Management. He is also the founder and director of the MIT Center for Coordination Science and one of two founding co-directors of the MIT Initiative on Inventing the Organizations of the 21st Century. Malone's research focuses on how new organizations can be designed to take advantage of the possibilities provided by information technology. Malone is known for having led the MIT team that developed the Information Lens system, a pioneering groupware tool in which intelligent agents help users find, filter, and sort large volumes of electronic information. Malone has been a co-founder of three software companies and has consulted and served as a board member for a number of other organizations.
