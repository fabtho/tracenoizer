The given names of William Lawton's daughters were Sarah, Elizabeth, and Abigail, with Sarah named first of the three daughters in his will. Although we do not know where William Lawton was living at the time of the Little Compton marriage, he did own land in Portsmouth, and could have been living there. If he was only eighteen years old when he was granted his brother Adam's land in Portsmouth, for which there are known to be precedents, he would have been eleven years older than Sarah Southworth. If he was twenty-one at that time, he would have been fourteen years older than Sarah, and about forty years of age when married. This puts them in the same age bracket as Sarah Southworth.
