London's Ken Livingstone became Britain's first elected mayor when he won as an independent in 2000, following an intensely bitter falling out with the Labour hierarchy which saw him kicked out of the party. . A key part of his winning formula was a promise to provide free bananas for schoolchildren. Following a second round of counting there, Conservative Chris Morgan beat off Labour's Eddie Darke to take the mayor's office by 26,083 votes to 24,531. There was humiliation in Watford, too, where Labour favourite Vince Muspratt lost out spectacularly to the Liberal Democrats.
