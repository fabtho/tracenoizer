Our studies of nonlinear propagation, deterministic chaos, and pattern formation in lasers are well known internationally, and unique in Australia and have resulted in 17 publications. Our optically pumped far-infrared NH3 laser exhibits Lorenz-like chaos, and produces some of the best chaotic data available, allowing in depth testing of theory and analysis methods. Our work is currently focussed on the effects of sudden parameter changes in chaotic systems and on means of controlling chaos by feedback. We are extending our work to the interactions of chaotic systems, such as synchronisation which has been proposed as the basis for secure communications. Other concerns are the interrelationship between chaotic and stochastic effects in real nonlinear systems.
