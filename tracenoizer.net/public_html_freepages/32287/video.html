.com "Hearth" 1995, Installation A metal sculpture with long thin legs holds up a steel house. When the door is opened the interior is visible which contains a small television monitor that plays a video fire. When I studied architecture I learned that the hearth was the focal center of the house and everything radiated out from this point. In many ways the television set has replaced this center so I brought the hearth back into the house via the monitor.
. This piece premiered last year and has been reworked for the festival junge hunde. The video is projected on the dancers and the floor, creating a continously changing space. It was designed and built by Nam June Paik and engineer, Shuya Abe, in 1969. We will be screening Jud Yalkut's historic tapes of the first Paik broadcasts as well as early programs made on the Paik-Abe by Bill Etra, Carol Goss, Shalom Gorewitz, Mary Ross and Reynold Weidenaar.
