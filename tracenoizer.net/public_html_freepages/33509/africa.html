The new governor Friedrich Lindequist (1805) supported the interests of the Settlers. General Lothar of Trotha, commander-in-chief of the German force, led the expedition corps to the suppression of the rebellion. Trotha had already gained experience in colonial conflicts and distinguished himself with extraordinary brutality in East Africa and China. The Herero were encircled on the so-called Waterberg by the forces and driven afterwards to the desert where most of them died. Trotha then gave the order, to kill all Herero, also women and children.
