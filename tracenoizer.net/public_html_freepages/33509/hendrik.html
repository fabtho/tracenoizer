As in general historical and social research, comparable efforts by Nama groups have received much less attention. Arguably the most important of these events has been for many years the event called Heroes Day and celebrated by the Witbooi group, generally in Gibeon. The date is late in October, to commemorate the October 25, 1905, when the national hero Kaptein Hendrik Witbooi was killed in action during the great Nama rising. The event was formerly called and still is widely known as Witbooi Festival or Witbooi Fees, and the change of designation forms an important part of the process reviewed here. Meanwhile, the observation of some salient date has become a practice for a number of further Nama groups in southern Namibia, and it can be surmised that this has spread from Gibeon.
