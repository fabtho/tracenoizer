Chris Johnson Chris Johnson's Home Page I'm Professor of Computing Science at Glasgow University. Research Full List of Publications and Recent Projects. Our work focusses on the interaction between failure in the operation, systems engineering, management and regulation of complex applications. I help to edit Interacting With Computers, Journal of Systems Safety and Reliability Engineering. I am organising two linked events for 2002, these are the 21st European Conference on Human Decision Making and Control and a Workshop on the Investigation and Reporting of Incidents and Accidents.
Director, Center for Bioelectric Field Modeling, Simulation, and Visualization (NIH NCRR). Co-Director, Advanced Visualization Technology Center (AVTC). Faculty Member, Computational Engineering and Science (CES) Program. Professor Johnson's research interests are in the area of scientific computing. Professor Johnson was awarded a Young Investigator's (FIRST) Award from the NIH in 1992, the NSF National Young Investigator (NYI) Award in 1994, and the NSF Presidential Faculty Fellow (PFF) award in 1995.
