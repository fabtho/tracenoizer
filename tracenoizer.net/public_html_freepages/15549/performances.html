The number of mergers and acquisitions has dramatically increased, especially between 1994 and 1996. The empirical analysis carried out in this paper tries to find out if mergers have increased banks' wealth or not. First of all, we examined differences between performances of the experimental group, made up of merged banks, and the control group (non-merged banks). Secondly, we compared merged banks' performances before and after the transaction. They differ passing from mergers through incorporation, to mergers through consolidation, to acquisitions.
