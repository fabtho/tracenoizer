Actually, I can't imagine why an artist who is working with computers isn't focusing on games. It's an arena that synthesizes technological and algorithmic advances at an astonishing rate. Critical to my thinking is that what I do is perceived as art, and not that I have made an art out of writing computer games. The later sentiment positions the work as something "other" when I fully intend it to be the thing itself, whatever that thing called art may be. I don't really want to start throwing up fences, but on the other side are artists who use the computer to simply engage in the current art dialogue.
