Examination of seedbed receptivity and careful ageing of white spruce in stands of varying ages indicated that the window of opportunity for recruitment following fire is about 3 years. In the years thereafter, white spruce occurrence is negatively impacted by falling logs, accumulation of litter, and development of grass cover. Occurrence of a mast seed year within two years of a burn had a dramatic effect on density of white spruce (2 to 4 times higher than sites with low seed production after fire). This mast seed year effect on density of white spruce could still be detected in stands 40 years post-fire. We hypothesise that conditions immediately following fire determine the occurrence, and density, of white spruce establishment on mixedwood sites and set stands on one of a few successional pathways, only one of which is likely to succeed to spruce-dominated.
