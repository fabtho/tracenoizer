Kirtland, USAF OFTEN THE lessons we learn best from combat come not from our victories but from our failures. Such a case is the Rail Interdiction Program, better known as Operation Strangle, conducted in Korea from the summer of 1951 to early 1952. Enthusiasm for air power and the desire to show that air power was enough to win a war were not enough to make up for deficiencies in planning, command structure, or resources that plagued this operation. The lessons those airmen learned so painfully, and at such a cost, forty years ago are still relevant today.
