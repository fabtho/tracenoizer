NCAEA History North Carolina Adult Education Association History Prior to the formation of a state organization, adult education in North Carolina was promoted through regonal and national conferences held in the state. Statistics on men rejected during World War I by the Armed Services because of illiteracy called for a response from adult educators in 1919. The American Association of Adult Education (later merged into AEA-USA) and the Southeastern Adult Education Association (SE-AEA) also met in North Carolina in 1929 and 1952 respectively.
