Policy on the Use of the University's Name, Seals, and Trademarks I. The names and seals have a unique value, and are afforded special protection by state law and University policy. Education Code Section 92000 conveys the unique value of the University's and Campus' name by making a violation of the section a misdemeanor. In addition to statutory protection, the University's and Campus' names and seals are protected by state and federal trademark law. The Campus' logos, designs, and visual images are also protected by trademark and copyright law.
