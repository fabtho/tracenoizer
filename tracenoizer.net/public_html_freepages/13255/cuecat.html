April 21, 2001 PDT The popular CueCat barcode scanner gives people quick access to Web information about products, but one digital artist wants to make sure consumers get a more complete picture. Consumers using the widely-distributed barcode reader and a PC can scan ads or articles in magazines and newspapers, and the CueCat automatically loads Web pages with detailed product information. The CueJack software gathers information from all kinds of sources, including news stories, pressure groups, consumer activists and disgruntled individuals.
