Its first tenant was the Bohemian Gymnastic Association, or Sokol. A Sokol, literally translated as "Falcon," was not merely a sports club. A Czech parallel to the German turnverein, it was actually a quasi-political organization. During the nineteenth century, the Czech lands were under the rule of the Austrian Empire and the Czech minority was prohibited from political participation. To circumvent this prohibition, as well as to foster nationalism the Sokol came into existence.
