In 1837, William Russell, a Virginian who made his fortune in real estate, purchased 76 acres to the south of the Soulard estate to be a country retreat called Crystal Springs Farm. Five years later he built a splendid stone house fronted by Doric columns and gave it to his only child, Ann and her new husband, Thomas Allen. Allen, an attorney who made his own fortune in railroad development, remained at the Farm for more than twenty years before the family moved to the swanky downtown Planter's Hotel. The house stood until nearly 1900 on the rise just to the northeast of the intersection of Ninth and Russell.
