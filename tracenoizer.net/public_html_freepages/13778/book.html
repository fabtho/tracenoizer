Back to Main Page FILM 187613 SECOND SERIES PAGE 133 WILL OF WILLIAM WATTS OF CRICK 1613 A note of William Watts his will taken the 4th day of Oct Anno Dom 1613 First I commit my soul unto Almighty Gods protection Item I give and bequeath unto my four daughters forty pounds apiece to be paid unto them when they shall accomplish the ages of 30 years or at the days of their marriage Item I give unto Richard Watts my son and to his heirs forever that half yard land which I purchased of my father, mason in Crick but my will is that the profits thereof be taken by my Executors from my decease till Michaelmas and from thence for four years following for the best performance of my will and legacies Item I give unto John Watts my son and to his heirs forever that quartern of land which I purchased of Mr Sherrock in Crick but my will is that my executors do take the profits thereof until Michaelmas and to that time for the space of five years after for the better performance of my will and legacies Also my will is that there shall be a surrender passed of my house and grounds according to their custom in Silsbie unto the use of William Watts my son and to his heirs forever and further my will is that my Executors take the rent and profit of that house and ground til Michaelmas and from thence for the space of eight years for the better performance of my will and legacies Item I give unto my son Jonas Watts twenty pounds to be paid unto him when he shall accomplish the age of 20 years Also my will is and I make my Executors Sibill my wife and Robert Watts my son; and my will is that my wife shall occupy and be half with Robert my son in all goods and increase that shall grow unto them during the time that she keepeth her self unmarried and have half all charges, but if my wife shall marry again then she shall have by reason of her joint Executorship or otherwise for her part but only twenty pounds and so depart. .
