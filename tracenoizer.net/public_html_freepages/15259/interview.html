Mr Fortuyn sent shock waves through the Dutch political scene after he and his followers won 17 of 45 council seats in March's municipal elections in Rotterdam with an aggressive nationalist and anti-immigrant platform. The May 15 parliamentary polls were seen as the next test of the far-right in Europe after the emergence of France's Jean-Marie Le Pen. The right-wing politician was shot after he finished a radio interview in the Media Park in Hilversum, 20 km from Amsterdam. A former academic and media commentator, he burst onto the political scene last year with a campaign to curb immigration. He directed his attacks at the Netherlands' growing Muslim community.
