The WPPSS nuclear plant would go into operation in 1977, and be followed by two more reactors at Hanford, and two at Satsop west of Olympia. It was the beginning of a disaster that would, for a time, threaten an economic meltdown of the Pacific Northwest. Seattle was smart enough to partly avoid the disaster and, in the process, begin rewriting the more-is-better doctrine that had defined "progress" in the region. By that time, WPPSS would be forced to halt work on four other partially completed nuclear plants. Seattle City Light was initially a leader and cheerleader in WPPSS, a public utility consortium that set out to build five nuclear plants at once.
