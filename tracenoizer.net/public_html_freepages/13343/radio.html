. Electromagnetic waves travel in a straight line or by reflection from the ionosphere or from a communications satellite. Because of their varying characteristics, radio waves of different lengths are employed for different purposes but are usually identified by their frequency (cycles per second). Radio waves range from a few kilohertz up to 30 gigahertz (30,000,000,000 Hz). The shortest waves have the highest frequency, or number of cycles per second; the longest waves have the lowest frequency, or fewest cycles per second.
