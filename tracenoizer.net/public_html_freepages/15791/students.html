. The Center coordinates and conducts extensive research related to the global water cycle, the biogeochemical cycles, Earth System history, and human impacts on the Earth system. Faculty and students cooperate closely in the planning and the execution of research projects, share expertise and insight into research problems, and work together on education and outreach programs. Since its creation in 1985, the Center has played a leading role in the early development of Earth system science as an academic discipline. .
