- career -

After graduation, he was drafted in the 40th round by the New York Mets in 1996, but he instead went to play for Centralia (Wash. He was again drafted in the 41st round by the Anaheim Angels in 1997. Again he opted for college play versus the majors and began a career at Portland State. As a Viking, he led the team in starts with 14 plus two complete games and 71 strikeouts. And to Arizona's advantage, Portland State lost its program, leading Pearce to a Wildcat career.
In the best of all worlds Richard would be an icon. Richard's father played the organ, a sister, the piano and a brother, the guitar. Possessed with a marvellous musical ear, Richard did some studies early in his career with reedmen such as Hugh Sealey, Herb Johnson and Arthur Romano. The late 50s found him doing a long stint with his childhood neighbour, pianist Oliver Jones. For a good part of the 70s he was mostly out of music working as a TV repairman.

Starting with his first performances about the troubles whilst at art school in Belfast in the seventies and his work with Tara Babel, through to his television appearances in the late-eighties and his most recent work to date, this publication charts Stitt's career using images from the artist's own archive as well as specially commissioned documentation. Stitt provides the context to his work from the last two decades with texts that link the various projects with his personal life, creating an invaluable document of an extreme and flamboyant career. .
