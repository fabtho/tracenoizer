Failing to fulfill energy delivery obligation may result in penalty to energy suppliers. This paper studies the relationship between generation level schedule, energy delivery schedule and physical ramp-rate of a generating unit. It is shown that although ramp-rate constraints are satisfied in traditional unit commitment problems, taking a generation level schedule as an energy delivery schedule may not be realizable.
., Bellevue, WA 98004; We describe mathematical modeling and implementation of the SPD application for the management of physical system operation in deregulated electricity markets. SPD analyzes bids for energy offers, reserve offers and energy demands and recognizes explicitly the effects on bid clearing due to transmission congestion, reserve requirements and other types of constraints. The generation system includes nuclear units, fossil-fired units and pumped storage plants. Several types of purchase and sale contracts are considered.
