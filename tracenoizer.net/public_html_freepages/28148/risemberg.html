With a Speedy, High-Tech Spin on Romance, the Matchmaking Service Date Bait Puts On the Pressure By DAPHNE UVILLER Forget the pretense of gallery talks, the forced cheeriness of mixers and the unwelcome surprises of blind dates. One of the latest phenomena to hit the Manhattan matchmaking scene, Date Bait, does away with all things genteel. Date Bait is a remarkably simple concept dreamt up four years ago by an Argentine Jew, Rafael Risemberg, who patented it last summer. Risemberg, now a 41-year-old education professor at Kean College in New Jersey, started Date Bait in October 1996 as a scheme for rustling up some dates for himself. Risemberg, who is gay, planned his first event for men who wanted to meet other men "as a self-interested experiment," he told the Forward.
