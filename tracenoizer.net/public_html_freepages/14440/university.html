With the help of several Graduate Students this course incorporates the best in traditional classroom teaching with advanced instructional technology. The 101 course is one of the four courses in the science literacy sequence of the new General Education Reform programs. The course was offered for the first time in the Fall of 1995 at Illinois State University, current enrollment is approximately 1200 students per semester. The objective of 101 course is to provide undergraduate students at Illinois State University with a better preparation for their understanding of themselves as biological creatures, the interaction between between the living world and the physical world. The course will help the students develop basic learning skills that will be necessary for pursuing a successful career in an ever changing job market.
