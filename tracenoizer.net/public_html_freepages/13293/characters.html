.org Cartoons Still Stereotype Gender Roles Males Portrayed as Doctors and Scientists, Females as Nurses and Bathing Beauties CHICAGO -- We haven't come that far, baby. In the 1970's male cartoon characters outnumbered female cartoon characters by a ratio of almost four to one. Research presented at the American Psychological Association's (APA) 105th Annual Convention in Chicago reveals that male cartoon characters still outnumber female cartoon characters almost four to one. Male characters are also still portrayed as dominate, powerful and aggressive.
