Another seating option that I like for distance paddling is to pull the Solostrap back from the center thwart another six inches and paddle with your paddle-side leg bent at the knee. Lock your bent knee in the corner where the center thwart meets the gunwale. For extra stability and that boot-like feel, keep your other leg stretched out and away from the paddle side, and flexed up against the center thwart. This knee up position relieves a lot of back strain. Being further away from the center, you'll want to move your load a little further forward for trim adjustment.
