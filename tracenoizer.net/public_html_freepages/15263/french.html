A BARBAROUS ACT Pim's MisfortuneA Dutch assassination weakens European democracy. The springtime of popular democracy in Europe went from surprising to terribly bloody yesterday in, of all places, the Netherlands. A day after the French peacefully turned away far-right presidential candidate Jean-Marie Le Pen at the ballot box, a gunman murdered the Dutch variety of that political genre, Pim Fortuyn. Dutch voters never got a chance to pass judgement on a man who briefly stirred up their country's staid political scene. Never before had a Dutch politician dared raise taboo subjects of immigration and crime so openly.
