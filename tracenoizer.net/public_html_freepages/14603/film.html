" --Art critic Jerry Saltz New York-based artist Matthew Barney has become well known for the unique combination of obsessive athletic endeavors with his own highly personalized mythology. Crossing from sculpture to film to video, he seamlessly creates dramas of compelling, compulsive force that inhabit a zone between the psychological and the physical. In this exhibition, the Walker Art Center presents the world premiere of Matthew Barney's latest film, Cremaster 2. Loosely based on the life of Gilmore as chronicled by Norman Mailer in his book The Executioner's Song, the film moves from a glacier field in the Canadian Rockies to the Bonneville Salt Flats, from Utah in 1976 to Chicago in 1893.
