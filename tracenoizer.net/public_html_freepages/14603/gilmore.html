For those who don't remember, Gary Gilmore was executed in 1976 for murdering two men on two consecutive nights. The case made history because it was the first execution in this country in ten years. More importantly, Gilmore refused the appeals process, demanding the right to die -- "blood atonement" in Morman doctrine -- for his sins.
