A soft systemic approach is being combined with multiple perspectives (Technical, Organisation, Personal) to provide a view of IS quality that is broader than traditional approaches to software quality. The research method involves the use of case studies and action research. This paper investigates the merits of the second-order-factor approach to IS Success measurement advocated in two recent papers. Second-order models assume that scores on all factors will tend to vary in unison, whereas first-order models make the less stringent assumption that IS Success is influenced by a number of independently-varying factors. -- Peter Seddon, (an empirical study of IS Success Measures), ACIS '96 .
