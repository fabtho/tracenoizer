Analyzing Costs of System-of-Systems Analyzing the Costs of System-of-Systems Year 2000 Problem Resolution John A. org ABSTRACT A major management challenge faced by every private and governmental organization is how to estimate the cost of fixing the Year 2000 date problem in interconnected systems. Up-front systems cost analyses for Year 2000 problem resolution must be sufficiently detailed for effective resource allocation and funding justification.
The framework includes a cost database, a cost model, and a process for collecting data. Standard cost estimating techniques and methods are applicable to the analysis of Year 2000 costs. The cost estimates must reflect both acquisition strategy issues and engineering assessments, as well as be conducted consistently across all the individual systems comprising the system-of-systems ensemble. Cost uncertainty at the system-of-systems level results from the propagation of uncertainties arising in individual systems. Additional uncertainties also arise from an incomplete but improving availability of input data, from continually increasing knowledge of all the impact areas, and from the changing count of systems affected as the Year 2000 rollover approaches.
