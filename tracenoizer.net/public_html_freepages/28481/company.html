It's difficult to resist free money and most people use the cards responsibly. The problem Nosik and UCMS have with credit-card companies is the way they bombard students with credit-card offers on campus and offer free gifts for applying without informing students of the responsibilities included with credit. One bill in California would make it illegal for any company to give away free gifts to applicants without accompanying it with educational material about credit. UCMS President Larry Chiang began the company in 1989 while a sophomore at the University of Iowa. He saw the large number of students getting credit cards through tables on campus and the need to inform students what it means to have good credit.
