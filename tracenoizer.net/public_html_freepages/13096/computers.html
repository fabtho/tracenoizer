Every agency and department of the Federal government is concerned with handling this potentially disastrous situation. Stated simply, the Y2K problem is one that arises historically from programmers using "00" as the designation for the year. Consequently, the use of only two digits results in most computers reading "00" as "1900". The General Services Administration and Presidential Council on Year 2000 Conversion both have websites to address the problem with standards and recommendations.
