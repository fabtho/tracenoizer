Superstructures Protein superstructures at exocytotic sites Thorsten Lang, Dietmar Riedel It is a major advantage of flat lawns of plasma membranes that the distribution of membrane proteins and membrane- associated proteins can be analyzed by fluorescence microscopy without any interference by labeled structures above or below the focal plane. Thus, unprecedented resolution of the distribution of proteins in the plane of the membrane can be achieved that is only limited by the resolutional limit of the light microscope. To our surprise we found that SNAREs and, in addition, other membrane constituents are not evenly distributed across the membrane surface but rather clustered in hot spots of high local protein density.
