. Requirements For The Masters In Social Work Degree Requirements For The Masters In Social Work Degree A total of 60 credits is required for the master's degree. Students in the 2-year program generally meet these requirements in 4 semesters of full-time study; students in the one-year residence program generally meet them in 5 semesters, plus two summers, of full and part-time study. In special circumstances, consideration can be given to other plans for completing the course of study. In their last semester, students are expected to complete a professional seminar which assists in the preparation of a major paper or project having as its central focus a practice issue of concern to them.
Major methods and second methods include classroom courses and field education. . MSW Program THE MASTER'S CURRICULUM The educational program leading to the Master of Social work degree requires students to complete a minimum of 60 credits consisting of 40 credits in classroom coursework and 20 credits in field education. Then, students select another practice concentration as their second method; including research which is available as a second method only. Major methods and second methods include classroom courses and field education.
