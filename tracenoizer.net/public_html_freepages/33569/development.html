First, instead of selling your past, try to get companies interested in things you want to do in the future. They may not get the ideal candidate pool, and this creates an opportunity to get them to rethink what they are looking for. For example, an electrical engineer may want to do business development or strategic marketing, rather than programming or project management. Companies simply need to hear the relevancy of your value to their business needs. Pretend you are hiring someone to be your next boss, or that you are essentially working for yourself.
