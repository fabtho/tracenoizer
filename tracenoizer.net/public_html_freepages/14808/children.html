One possible scenario is to use the browser's filter and have its default setting at a minimal level on PCs in the adult area. This may eliminate most adult patron requests to have the filter disabled. The library could then have more stringent settings or another filtering program operational on PCs in the children's area. The problematic nature of this disabling option on PCs in areas open to the public is referenced by the ALA in its lawsuit. The law states that technology protection measures must be on any computers with Internet access, to protect against access to visual depictions referenced in the act.
Ongoing evaluation and monitoring by a physician is essential. Parents and guardians should be provided with complete information when psychiatric medication is recommended as part of their child's treatment plan. Children and adolescents should be included in the discussion about medications, using words they understand. What is known about its helpfulness with other children who have a similar condition to my child. What are the side effects which commonly occur with this medication.
