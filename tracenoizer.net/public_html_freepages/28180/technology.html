Computer-aided process planning initially evolved as a means to electronically store a process plan once it was created, retrieve it, modify it for a new part and print the plan (Stage II). Other capabilities of this stage are table-driven cost and standard estimating systems. However, variant CAPP is based on a Group Technology (GT) coding and classification approach to identify a larger number of part attributes or parameters. These attributes allow the system to select a baseline process plan for the part family and accomplish about ninety percent of the planning work. The planner will add the remaining ten percent of the effort modifying or fine-tuning the process plan.
