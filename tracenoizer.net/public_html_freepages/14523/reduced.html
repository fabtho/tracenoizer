The research project for the Guardian amongst swing voters in Leeds, Edgware in north west London and Northampton, confirms that Labour's huge lead in the opinion polls masks increasing unease amongst the electorate particularly over health and transport. The results of six focus groups convened just before the party conference season show that amongst these key voters the Conservatives are making some modest progress and William Hague is managing to recover some ground in convincing the electorate that he is reforming his party. But they share the common view that the Tories will not win the next election, although Labour will have a reduced majority. And although Mr Hague is seen as having "grown up a bit", nobody could name anyone in the shadow cabinet. The names that cropped up instead included Chris Patten, Edwina Currie, and Michael Portillo.
