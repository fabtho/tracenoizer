After returning to Sweden in 1889, Hedin studied geography and geology at the Universities of Uppsala and Berlin. Hedin was blinded in the early 1890s from one eye, and suffered from it until he was 82 - after an operation the sight was restored. Hedin's doctoral thesis was entitled DER DEMAVEND NACH EIGENER BEOBACHTUNG.
Together with Stevie B-Zet, he produced the soundtrack of the thriller The Cold Finger which opened in may throughout German movie theatre's. Sven separated from his business partners and left EYE-Q. Around June '97 after moving to Berlin the German label wasn't able to pay their bills and filed a petitition for bankruptcy. EYE-Q UK is still alive and releasing new records, but this has nothing to do with Sven and the 'good old days'. At the end of '97 the rights on the name HARTHOUSE has been sold to UCMG (Undercover Music Group).
