After a year of producing two excellent examples of how country music should be sung, Decca's closing left some of us on the 'Row wondering where she might be going. MCA Records, the company that acquired Decca, realized very early Lee Ann still had a vast amount of talent that needed to be given the chance to grow. Her third CD, 'I Hope You Dance', is one of the finest musical offerings to come out of Nashville in quite a while. The songs on this CD cover almost every feeling on the emotional spectrum, and her voice never skips a note as she leads you down the path of heartfelt discovery. Songs like 'Ashes By Now' and 'The Healing Kind' give anyone who hears them reasons to see the heartbreak in the the world around them.
