The apoptotic deletion of activated T cells has been proposed as the key mechanism to maintain T cell homeostasis, and in this respect CD95 (fas antigen) seems to play a major role in this course of events. This study reports our analysis on 140 healthy individuals arranged in different groups starting on cord blood to 91 years old. Our results show that in the course of growing, just collected lymphocytes, show a progressive increase of expression of the apoptosis molecule CD95. At 60 years of age until 91 the expression of CD95 reaches the higher level. The expression of CD95 fits well with the spontaneous apoptosis observed in the different groups.
