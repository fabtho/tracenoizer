No family intervention should be undertaken without advice and counsel of a professional experienced in the intervention process and, except in rare situations, no family intervention should be undertaken without the professional being present at critical times. Furthermore, since people embarking on an intervention often feel ambivalent and apprehensive, it is important that they trust the interventionist. Should you ever feel uneasy with your interventionist, that you are being asked to do something you do not understand or agree with, you would be wise to stop the process and go elsewhere.
