Self-assembly is a term that describes the spontaneous formation of ordered structures, usually on mesoscopic scales, as a result of competing interactions that are usually present in complex fluids. The complex hierarchical organization in biological systems is largely due to self-assembly. Therefore, a fundamental understanding of self-assembly holds the key to understanding the myriad of wonderful forms that we find in Nature. Equally important, the principles of self-assembly can be profitably exploited in the design and synthesis of new materials.
