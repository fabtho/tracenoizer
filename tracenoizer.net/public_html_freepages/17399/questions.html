Each IRG study section is assisted by a scientific review administrator (SRA) who is the designated Federal official responsible for conducting the grant application review in an impartial environment. For purposes of clarity, IRG study sections will be referred to as IRGs in this publication. They are also responsible for attending board or council meetings to provide requested information in support of committee recommendations, communicating with program staff on review issues, and discussing review questions with applicants. SRAs do not have continuing programmatic, scientific, or fiscal responsibilities after the review is completed. For any applications referred to NCI for review that cannot be reviewed by an IRG for reasons of conflict of interest or lack of expertise, Special Emphasis Panels (SEPs) (formerly Special Review Committees) are assembled for the review.
