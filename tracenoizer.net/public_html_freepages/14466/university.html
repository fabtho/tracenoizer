Johnson joined the Mustang program in the summer of 2000 and his focus is on the increasing administrative demands of the the program. His duties include assisting in scheduling, monitoring players' academic progress, organization of team travel, time management of team and office organization. Johnson came to SMU from NCAA Division II Northwest Missouri State University in Maryville, Mo. , where he served as an assistant coach since 1994, following his playing career for the Bearcats. He played guard for the Bearcats from 1988-92, scoring 669 points in his career, and was a two-time Mid-America Intercollegiate Athletics Association All-Academic selection.
More recently we have installed an Aviv 202 circular dichroism spectrometer with a prototype stopped-flow system. This uses a four syringe stepper motor configuration allowing single or double mixing. We have a commercially manufactured temperature jump instrument from DiaLog as well as a pressure jump instrument built to a design of Professor Freddie Gutfreund at the University of Bristol. Circular dichroism (the difference in absorption of left and right circularly polarized light) is used to follow changes in the chirality of molecules.
