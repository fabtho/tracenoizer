In a letter to WSWS arts editor David Walsh writer John Nichols, author of The Sterile Cuckoo, contributed this recollection of Alan Pakula. In 1965 or '66 Alan Pakula hired me to write a screenplay of The Sterile Cuckoo. I went to LA a couple of times and spent long sessions with him and Bob Mulligan (director of To Kill a Mockingbird, which Alan produced). Alan was a warm, gentle, intelligent, amazingly hard-working and personable guy. I was surely impressed, as it immediately queered any prejudices about stereotypical Hollywood that I might have harbored.
