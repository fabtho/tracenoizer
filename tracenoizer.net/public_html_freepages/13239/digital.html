. Video here is pared back to less than zero - yet despite their ultra-minimalist outline, these four pieces imprint themselves vividly on the mind. The ability of sound to conjure its own evocative visual landscape is a feature of many of the works, whose repeated emphasis on dialogue (between disembodied voices, between audience and artwork, or between the gallery and an off-site location) encourages a more subtle and intuitive form of interactivity than that promoted by push-button digital technology. With their accent on relationships, networks and communities, these four pieces sound a welcome note of humanity within an encroaching and occasionally alienating digital world.
