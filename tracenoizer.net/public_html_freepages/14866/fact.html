Both of the figures discussed here have succeeded in provoking a great deal of ire and controversy. Yet for all this, outsiders are often greeted with outright hostility. Much of this might be interpreted as a result of the fact that they come from outside the discipline of Classical Archaeology. The question remains whether the strength of the reactions was due to professional jealousy or dismay at the untrained, potentially destructive methods of these non-professional outsiders. Heinrich Schliemann (1822-1890) is famous for his twin discoveries of Troy and, later, at Mycenae.
