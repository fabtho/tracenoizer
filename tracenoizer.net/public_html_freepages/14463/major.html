Johnson, Charles Spurgeon Johnson, Charles Spurgeon Charles Spurgeon Johnson Fisk University News Bureau (b. (established in 1867 and long restricted to black students). Earlier he had founded and edited (1923-28) the intellectual magazine Opportunity, a major voice of the Harlem Renaissance of the 1920s. After graduation from Virginia Union University, Richmond, Johnson studied under the sociologist Robert Ezra Park at the University of Chicago and then worked for the Chicago Commission on Race Relations (1919-21). His first important writing, The Negro in Chicago (1922), was a sociological study of the race riot in that city in July 1919.
