The Scott Polar Research Institute's use of remote sensing methods in glaciology began in 1963 with the development of the first radio echo-sounding (RES) equipment for the measurement of ice thickness. RES is a radio-frequency (VHF or UHF) technique, depending on the transparency of ice at these frequencies, normally operated from the ice surface or from an airborne platform. In 1967 a joint programme of airborne radio echo-sounding of the Antarctic ice sheet was begun by SPRI and the US National Science Foundation, culminating in the publication in 1983 of the Antarctic Folio which contains a detailed description of about half of the ice sheet of east Antarctica.
