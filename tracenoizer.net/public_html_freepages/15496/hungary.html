Hungarian foreign policy is particularly sensitive about the treatment of Hungarian minorities in neighboring Romanian Transylvania, Serbian Vojvodina, and Slovakia. . Lying at the heart of central Europe, Hungary is landlocked and has borders with seven states. Historically, Hungary has been a cosmopolitan cultural center, and during its years of market socialism was more prosperous than the other Eastern Bloc countries. Hungary's economic and political reforms have brought it closer to the EU and NATO membership.
The people are largely ethnic Hungarians; Gypsies are the largest minority (5. About two thirds of the population is Roman Catholic, but there is a large Protestant minority and nearly 100,000 Jews (the largest Jewish population in central Europe). Budapest city (1990 population 2,016,100) is the capital and largest city of Hungary on both banks of the Danube River. The area may have been settled in the Neolithic era. Under Roman rule from the 1st century AD, the cities were destroyed by the Mongols in 1241.
