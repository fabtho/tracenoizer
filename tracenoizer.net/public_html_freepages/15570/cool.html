The graphic at the top of this page shows how heat bursts form. After sunset, warm, moist air feeding a thunderstorm shuts off and the storm collapses. All the rain in the storm's top falls into cool, dry air charging into the storm from the back side. As the rain falls, it evaporates in the dry air and cools further making it very heavy. This cool, heavy air plunges toward the ground in a giant, downward-moving burst.
