Finally, users may enable some services - such as file sharing - without realising the consequences of offering them to the whole world. Many of these problems can be prevented by blocking, or restricting, these services at the router or firewall that connects a local area network to the public Internet. As this is a "default-permit" approach it cannot protect against new attacks and should not be regarded as a long term measure, however a number of sites have reported a considerable decrease in security incidents as a result of restricting access from the Internet to a very small number of services.
