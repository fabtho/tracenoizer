Adee almost singlehandedly created a lively and aggressive sports section that competed favorably against the Tribune's. He supplied an abundance of the section's enterprise ideas, nursed his columnists' egos, and earned the devout loyalty of his staff. I am less familiar with Winnecke's role at the paper, but she is said to be a strong leader and talented editor; she continued to run the paper's daily news meetings after Cooke and Cruickshank came aboard. She also represented management in union negotiations and handled administrative matters involving personnel and budgeting. Amazingly, Adee, Winnecke, and the Tribune managed to keep their months-long negotiations out of the rumor mill.
