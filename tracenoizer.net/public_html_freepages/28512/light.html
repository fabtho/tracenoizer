One of my early assignments was to extract a recovery schedule from a physicist whose project had slipped past its scheduled completion date. The project was to develop a flash blindness prevention visor for a pilot who might witness a nuclear explosion. The visor was made of a light-sensitive plastic (now available in eyeglasses). The problem was that the required response time exceeded the limitations of the material being used. At first, the physicist was happy to share his problem and data that reinforced how difficult the problem was.
Synapomorphies of Discoglossanura include a bicondylar sacrococcygeal articulation and the presence of an episternum. Discoglossanura has two subgroups, Discoglossidae and Pipanura. However, her sampling of taxa is not sufficient to provide strong support for this clade, especially in light of the synapomorphies supporting other arrangements. The subordinal name Ranoidei was coined for this clade by Sokol (1977), but that name was re-assigned to a less inclusive taxon by Dubois (1983, 1984). Sokol's (1977) use of this name was unfortunate because the informal name, ranoid, is homonymous with the widely used name Ranoidea.
