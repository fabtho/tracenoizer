Embracing the non-linear quality of the Internet, the nine net art projects chosen for the 2000 Biennial use an entirely new vocabulary of forms and methods, taking advantage of the seemingly random free association of the Internet. These projects balance a mastery of technology with sophisticated visual and conceptual concerns, blurring boundaries between numerous previously distinct disciplines. "Internet art has reached a critical stage where a significant number of artists are producing works for this new medium. An impressive number of really exciting works have been made, and a substantial critical dialogue has developed that is slowly but surely drawing in mainstream art historians and theorists.
