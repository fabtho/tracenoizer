News Feature story - April 11, 2001 At rest in Mexico Community pitches in to ensure that a restaurant worker is buried by his family. By Melissa Davidson Carmelo Corona Pena is resting in peace in his hometown of San Tadeo, Huiluapan, Tlaxcala, Mexico. A tangled web of paperwork nearly prevented friends and Latino community members from getting the body to Mexico City early Saturday in time for the funeral. About 10 family members were waiting at the airport to pick up their loved one and give him a proper burial in his village. Pena's boss at Harvest Bakery and Health Foods and his friends in Jackson weren't about to let complications get in the way of a proper funeral for the cook of more than two years.
