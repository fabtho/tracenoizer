In 1768 Goethe was stricken by a severe illness, forcing the termination of his studies at Leipzig and the return to his home in Frankfurt. After a long period of convalescence, Goethe was sent by his father to earn a degree in law at the University of Strassburg. On August 6 of 1771, Goethe was awarded his degree following the successful defense of Positiones juris, a twelve-page dissertation containing fifty-six maxims on the nature and function of law. Goethe soon returned home to begin a career as an advocate but instead became a leader in the literary revolt then brewing against the principles of the Enlightenment.
