North Dakota Department of Human Services - Child Support Enforcement - Home North Dakota's Child Support Enforcement program is a joint effort of federal, state, and county governments to secure support payments for children whose parents are not both in the home. Its goals are (1) to ensure that children are supported by their parents; (2) to foster family responsibility; and (3) to reduce the cost of welfare. The program has the responsibility of implementing Title IV-D of the Social Security Act in the state.
