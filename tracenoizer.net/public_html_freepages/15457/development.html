This summer, a team of archaeologists from three British Universities has been trying to establish whether the geophysical features really represented lost stones. The excavation has also uncovered a ditched enclosure that almost certainly pre-dates the stone Avenue and may provide valuable clues about the early development of the Avebury complex. First identified on an aerial photograph, the excavation has established the earlier Neolithic date of this ditch, and that it was built from intersecting segments rather than as a continuous ditch. "The rediscovery of a major megalithic monument (the Beckhampton Avenue) has profound implications for our understanding of the Avebury complex and Neolithic society" said Dr Joshua Pollard (University of Wales, Newport) "We now know that, during the later Neolithic period, the scale of monument building in the Avebury area was much greater than had been previously suspected.
