. Our goals are both to do original research which can be published in the open literature, and to see the results of our work used in technologies that are of benefit to users. We are interested in collaborating with outside academic or industrial partners in pursuit of either or both of these goals. What we do Our work is mainly in the areas of speech recognition and synthesis. The results of this research are reported in our publications and implemented in useful software, like the Festival speech synthesis system.
Against the background of Germany's 20th century history, but with particular emphasis on the past four decades, students will study Germany's growth from the complete destruction of 1945 to the democratic, cultural and economic strength of today. Materials to be studied include historical and topical documents, film, video and web-based resources. Depending upon academic area of interest, there will be opportunity for students to pursue and present findings on an individual topic selected from a wide variety of fields. Second year standing in German language, or equivalent, is advisable, but not absolutely necessary (see instructor). The essay must conform to acceptable standards of style and format and be submitted before the end of classes.
