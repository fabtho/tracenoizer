The limbic system is very close by and is closely wired to the sensory systems. Whatever parts of that central nervous system are not working correctly are going to cause clinical manifestations. Why is this so difficult for most of the medical community to accept than other necrologic diseases. It's very easy to measure the neurological malfunction if you have Parkinson's Disease because it affects one of the motor control centers. Fibromyalgia and chronic fatigue are also control center malfunctions, just a different one --a much more difficult one to measure.
