. March 28, 2001 Second Study Questions Research Linking Voucher Threat to Gains By Jessica L. Sandham Debate continued last week over a recent report that connected gains in student achievement in Florida to the state's voucher program, as another scholar came forward to question the conclusions of the Manhattan Institute study. Greene's report, "An Evaluation of the Florida A-Plus Accountability and School Choice Program," is available from the Manhattan Institute for Policy Research. In an academic article published on March 19, a professor at the University of Colorado at Boulder contends that the substantial gains on state tests achieved by Florida schools facing the threat of vouchers might be due to schools' efforts to achieve minimum passing scores on the writing portion of the exam.
