Man is subjected by modern war to a nervous tension, a psychic pressure, and an animal submission which are beyond human power to support. But, involved and committed to the machine, he does contrive to support all this, admirable machine that he is. In the process, however, he is stretched to the limit of his resistance, like a steel cable which may break at any moment. The conditions of war may still be abnormal and exceptional. Nevertheless, even four or five years of war are significant in the life of a man.
