Dirichlet proved case 1 and presented his paper to the Paris Academy in July 1825. The complete proof was published in September 1825. On 28 November 1825 General Foy died and Dirichlet decided to return to Germany. He was encouraged in this by Alexander von Humboldt who made recommendations on his behalf. In 1455, when the loan payment came due again, Fust repossessed the printing company and the presses and went into business with Peter Schoeffer, Gutenberg's protege and a relative of Fust's. Gutenberg set up another printing company in Mainz with the help of Konrad Humery. Many printers left Mainz at that time and spread the printing technology and skills throughout Germany and then to the rest of Europe. Gutenberg stayed in Mainz and petitioned the Church court for a secured position. It is unclear as to whether he continued his printing activities after 1465.
