Since mid-century the citizens of the United States have been history 's most generous patrons of fundamental scientific research. As the federal commitment to scientific research grew rapidly after World War II, about 50 universities and institutes constructed laboratories and enlarged their science faculties, producing a research enterprise that is the envy of the world. Across the broad spectrum of the sciences from physics to biology the advances have been spectacular, fueling the economy through technical innovation and improving our quality of life through improved medical care and new consumer products.
