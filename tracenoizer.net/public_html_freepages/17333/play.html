John Holt is an author and expert in the childhood learning process. A weakness of traditional education, he feels, is that children are required to spend years memorizing facts that hold no interest for them. Alternatively, children left to learn through fantasy and play are able to gather information at their own pace and under their own conditions. When left alone, Holt maintains, children are capable of complicated games that would baffle children in a classroom situation.
