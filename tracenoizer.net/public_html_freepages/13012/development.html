. Miskovic joined ICN Pharmaceuticals in 1993 and most recently served as Vice President of Legal for ICN's European operations. Prior to that position, he was ICN's Vice President of Corporate Development in Eastern Europe. Miskovic's career began in the field of government, serving as General Secretary in the Federal Parliament of Yugoslavia. During my student days at Concordia I worked in microelectronics laboratory on many very interesting projects with very nice people. Since June 1999 I have been working for Trex Medical Inc. My work is in research and development of X-ray equipment for cardiovascular imaging. If you want to see something about what I am doing there click here. In addition to my everyday job, since fall 1997 I was a part time master degree student in electrical engineering.
I was born and grew up in Yugoslavia , where I also earned a B. degree in electrical engineering from University of Belgrade, Belgrade. My research concerns development and application of control theoretic methods for increased performance of switched reluctance motors (SRM). The SRM has many advantages over other types of electric motors, including constructional simplicity, low cost, high degree of fault tolerance and efficiency. However the acceptance of SRM drives in industrial applications is limited in large part by simplistic control strategies which do not yield acceptable levels of torque and speed ripple, and by noise produced by the motor.
