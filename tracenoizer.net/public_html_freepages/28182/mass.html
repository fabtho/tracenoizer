Wind blows from West to East, therefore, our wind blows moist air from the Pacific Ocean, which is forced up by the Cascade Mountains. Frontal precipitation is caused when a warm air mass (front) or a cold air mass (front) meet each other causing one to uplift and go over the other. Because cold air is usually denser than warm air it tends to move under the warm air mass. A cold front meeting a warm front usually is intense than when a warm front advances on a cold front.
