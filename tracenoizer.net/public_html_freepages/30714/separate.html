. It was not until late in the nineteenth century that companies began to be seen as something wholly different from partnerships. It was little more than a hundred years ago that the separate legal personality of a company fully emerged and company law doctrines, separate and distinct from partnership doctrines, finally developed. At the same time, shareholders now had rights and responsibilities that were different from those enjoyed by partners. In more recent years, especially as public companies have become larger and more powerful, the role of shareholders and the analysis of the corporate form itself have been further modified.
