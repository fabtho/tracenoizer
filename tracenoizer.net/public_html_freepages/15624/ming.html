Synopsis After Manchu invaders breached the Great Wall of China to establish the Qing dynasty in the 17th century, Zheng Cheng Gong (known as Koxinga in the West) fled to the south where Emperor Longwu had relocated the Ming dynasty. Appointed to head the Ming army, Zheng Cheng Gong led his coastal forces in resistance for several years before sailing for Taiwan, then a sparsely populated outpost supporting some Dutch traders and a small garrison. Two decades earlier the Dutch had inflicted a humiliating defeat on the Chinese and had occupied the island of Taiwan.
