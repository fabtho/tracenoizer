Thermopower looks at the response of electrons to a temperature gradient, and provides complementary information to the conventional resistance. From measurements of the thermopower across a 1D wire we have developed a mesoscopic thermometer that can accurately measure the electron temperature. Samples for this project will be available in a collaboration with the Semiconductor Physics group at the Cavendish Laboratory (University of Cambridge), where there are extensive growth and fabrication facilities. A 3He system and associated measuring equipment is being brought to Royal Holloway and will be set up in the first few months of 2001, so that experiments can commence in the Spring. Dr Nicholls has over ten years hands-on experience in low-noise measurements at low temperatures, and will be joined by a post-doctoral research assistant who will be starting on this project in October 2001.
