The first visit is to the Malay Quarter - an area near the city center where the original slaves were kept. Today the main inhabitants are Cape Muslims, who have their own origins in the groups of slaves and political exiles, brought to the southern tip of Africa during the 17th century. On the tour you learn about the slave history, see the beautiful cobblestone streets, colorful houses and the oldest mosque in the area. Then continue on to District Six - once home to thousands of Capetonians of all hues and cultures. In the 1960's, these people were forcibly removed from their homes and scattered about the Cape to areas known as the Cape Flats.
