. The visitors explore the space of this room and can activate the intertwining wreaths of autonomous sounds. By means of a helmet, the user is integrated into the computerized system where the sounds all have symbolic presence. These sounds organize themselves, according to their similarities, inside a sound database. Each sound has a particular signature which it is possible to identify.

. The visitor can explore different modes of group modalities of interaction and differing linkage between real and virtual crowds. Based on events in real space, that get recognized using a vision system, corresponding events in virtual space are triggered. Our findings give some insight in the social and artistic perspective of the approach. Vision tracking hardware and software technology has been applied on a situation in a dome projection system where 5 persons interact with the projected imagery of a virtual deep-sea world in which schools of marine life forms roam the grounds.
