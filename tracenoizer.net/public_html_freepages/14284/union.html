Erkki Liikanen was appointed as Member of the European Commission responsible for enterprise and information society. Liikanen served (from 1995 to 1999) as Member of the European Commission, responsible for budget issues, personnel and internal administration. Liikanen was the Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary from Finland to the European Union. During that period Finland negotiated the Accession to the European Union. Liikanen was elected to the Finnish Parliament in 1972 at the age of 21.

Telecom Package Mr Erkki Liikanen, member of the European Commission, responsible for Enterprise and the Information Society, voiced his remarks regarding the Telecom Package in the European Parliament. The European Union now faces a new regulatory framework for electronic communications. Liikanen believes that it constitutes an important base of the European policy since the Union wishes to build 'the most successful knowledge-based economy in the world'. The package is supposed to boost a slow-working sector since unnecessary regulation should be excluded.
