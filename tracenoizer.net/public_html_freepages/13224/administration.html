The Parliament of Canada Act delegates the management of this organization to the Board of Internal Economy, chaired by the Speaker of the House. . In doing so, the administration provides a wide range of services -- from procedural advice and financial guidance to secure, well-maintained buildings and technological tools. As custodian of historical records and impartial advisor on procedures, the administration works through the myriad traditions, rules and procedures of the House to assist Members in their work in Chamber. The administration also promotes knowledge and understanding of the House of Commons and Parliament by supporting the domestic and international work of Members through a range of educational programs, as well as by making information accessible to Canadians and others.
