Created in response to the animation success of Disney in the 1930s, the lovable Looney Tunes characters have continued to define and reflect popular American culture over the past sixty years. As a commentary on the Looney Tune's critical role in popular art, New York's Museum of Modern Art hosted a full-scale retrospective for four-and-a-half months in Fall, 1985. Devoting gallery space to original drawings and weekend theater screens to the reshowing of the original films, MoMa restated a modern phenomena--the significant cultural, theoretical, and artistic contribution of the Loony Tunes as legitimate American contemporary art.
