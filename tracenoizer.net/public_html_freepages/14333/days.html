Programmes of preparation for nurses, midwives and health visitors to prescribe from the Nurse Prescribers' Extended Formulary began in January 2002, and the first nurses are expected to qualify in April 2002. All nurses, midwives and health visitors carrying out extended independent prescribing responsibilities will undergo a programme of preparation at degree level (level three in England). The programme will include 25 taught days in the university plus 12 nurse days when a designated medical practitioner will provide the student with supervision, support and opportunities to develop competence in prescribing practice. There will be assessment of theory and prescribing competence. The programme will be part-time spread over a period of three months.
