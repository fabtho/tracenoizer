The visit to South Africa took, as its focus, business and management programmes. This report is one of a number of reports published from the summer 1999 overseas audit programme to South Africa. The audit examined the policies and procedures used by the University to satisfy itself of the academic quality and standards of its awards being offered in South Africa. The programmes covered by this partnership were a post experience programme which operates under the Integrated Manager Development Scheme (IMDS) and leads to a post experience certificate or diploma in Engineering Business Management; a master's programme leading to the MSc in Engineering Business Management which uses the operational title of Integrated Graduate Development Scheme (IGDS); and the Engineering Doctorate (EngD). The programmes provide progression for candidates where appropriate.
Comparability is promoted across all delivery locations through the application of an agreed set of norms. The external examiner looks at those scripts lying outside of the expected norms and is also provided with a statistical report prior to the examination board. Noting that the role of a moderator could mean scrutinising in excess of 300 scripts, University teaching staff explained that the task was a particularly onerous one undertaken in addition to existing responsibilities. From the information made available to it, the team concluded that the standards of the awards were appropriate. The database is made available to partner organisations on a commercial basis.
