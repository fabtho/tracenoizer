The patterns of the dances represent the contours of human organs of reproduction. CREMASTER 1 is a striking film sculpture on mass and ornament, a metaphor of reproduction and sexual indifference, the history of a system in suspense. Matthew Barney creates a link between film, dance and visual art in this piece. He draws from mythology as well as from the cyberspace of the nineties. Within only a few years Barney has undergone a meteoric development, participating in both the documenta IX and in the Whitney Biennial.
Matthew Barney won the prestigious Europa 2000 prize at the 45th Venice Biennale in 1996. . Biography Matthew Barney was born in San Francisco in 1967; at age six, he moved to Idaho with his family. This intermingling of sports and art informs his work as a sculptor and filmmaker. After graduating from Yale in 1991, Barney entered the art world to almost instant controversy and success.
Solo exhibitions of his work have been held at the Tate Gallery, London; the Cartier Foundation for Contemporary Art, Paris; and the Museum Boymans-van Beuningen, Rotterdam. The latter exhibition travelled to the cpac MusE d'Art Contemporain, Bordeaux, and is currently on view in the Kunsthalle Bern. Barney was awarded the Europa 2000 prize for his contribution to the Aperto of the 1993 Venice Biennale. ..
