Infinite Dimensional Stochastic Analysis Infinite Dimensional Stochastic Analysis (includes Malliavin Calculus, Dirichlet Forms) November 3-7, 1997 Video for this conference is available in our Video Archive. There are challenging connections between this calculus and other areas of stochastic analysis such as diffusions on manifolds, anticipating stochastic calculus and quantum field theory. The theory of Dirichlet forms provides analytic tools to construct and to study a large class of Markov processes with irregular or large state spaces like fractals or infinite-dimensional manifolds. The purpose of this workshop is to review the current state of knowledge in these areas, to identify important directions for future research, and to provide a forum for cross-fertilization of ideas between the two areas.
