He has over 220 publications to his name and has been the recipient of numerous awards, including the degree of Docent from Stockholm University and a Fellowship of the Royal Society of Edinburgh. Drawing upon his 30 years worth of expertise in the function and physiology of the "powerhouses" of the cell, known as mitochondria, Dr. Nicholls is attempting to understand why nerve cells die as a result of stroke and other age-related diseases such as Parkinson's disease, Huntington's disease and Alzheimer's disease. While it is clear that damaged or malfunctioning mitochondria play a central role in the processes of cell death, the exact relationship between cell death and mitochondrial activity is still being established. Nicholls's research has concerned a process known as excitotoxicity, which has been implicated in many of the major neurodegenerative diseases.
