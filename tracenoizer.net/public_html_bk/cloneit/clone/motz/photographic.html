Above all because of the influences of the New American Topographics in the Seventies, the topic of landscape can no longer be separated from nature being invaded by civilization. Talking about landscape or rather topography also stands for images dealing about the links between nature and the areas used or built by man. One of the features of contemporary German photography is that the response to photographic concepts has polarised itself in a very peculiar way. While in the Seventies there still was the belief in a democratic break-up of the technical medium in favour of a new shere of artistic acceptance, there only seems to be left a minor application of this today. Even with the medium having gained a considerable reputation, the presence of photography in art museums and on the art market reflects this discrepancy.
