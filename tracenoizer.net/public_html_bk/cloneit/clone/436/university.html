INTRODUCTION Overall institutional excellence requires a high level of quality in those operations which support the primary academic units and serve the basic personal needs of students, and the following review procedure is designed to assess operations of the Student Services offices which provide such support. Since it was originally written in 1975, it has been developed and revised by a committee consisting of Student Services directors based on comments and recommendations submitted by other Student Services directors and members of the University administration. Background Throughout its history, The University of Iowa has been committed to academic excellence through continued review of all levels of function. In 1986-87, a University Self-Study was completed and its report, Building on Strength, proposed ambitious aspirations for the University and suggested a number of steps for moving toward them.

Rodger Martin - Biographical Information Rodger Martin Biographical Information Professional Profile Dr. Martin is Group Leader for Californium Program Development and Analysis at the Radiochemical Engineering Development Center (REDC) of the Chemical Technology Division, Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL), and the founding Director of the Californium User Facility for Neutron Science at ORNL since 1997. He is responsible for customer liaison in supplying Californium-252 sources and provides administrative support, university liaison, and strategic direction for Californium Program initiatives. Martin was awarded an ORNL seed money grant which reactivated the Cf-252 brachytherapy source fabrication program, after termination of operations at Savannah River Laboratory and several years of dormancy, and currently provides the administrative lead for the ORNL Cf-252 medical source initiative. Specific responsibilities include evaluation of capsule design, internal pressure calculations, and regulations for source special form testing and transportation.
