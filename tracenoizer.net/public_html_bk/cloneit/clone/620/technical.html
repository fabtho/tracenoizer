As one possibility out of many tele-learning solutions virtual seminars are developing towards a wide-spread and accepted instructional choice in universities and adult education. The first evaluative studies report problems as well. HESSE and GIOVIS (1997) state low participation rates, REIMANN (1998) mentions technical difficulties concerning hard- and software, NISTOR and MANDL (1995) report the heavy load on cognitive resources and UTZ and SASSENBERG (1999) found high drop-out rates as central points of difficulty. UTZ and SASSENBERG (1999) showed, too, that high identification with a virtual seminar proves to be a solution for some of the above mentioned problems.
