May Eliza Wright Sewall (1844-1920) Educator and reformer May Eliza Wright Sewall was a graduate of Northwestern Female College (later absorbed by Northwestern University). From 1872 to 1880 she was a high school teacher in Indianapolis. During her years in education she also became widely known for her efforts in the women's rights movement. In 1888 Sewall organized a convention in Washington, DC to mark the 40th anniversary of the Seneca Falls Convention. From the Washington meeting emerged the National Council of Women, of which Sewall served first as recording secretary and later as president.
