Proceedings of the First NSF International Conference on Food Safety. . Schwarz joined the Fisheries and Wildlife Sciences faculty at Virginia Tech in 1997. His Masters work centered on design and implementation of a thermal refuge, which was placed within outdoor production ponds to protect cultured fish from winter cold-kill. Applications of this technology are now in place at numerous facilities along the Gulf Coast.
He joined the Queens College Department of Physics in 1994, where his research continues in a similar vein. He is the author of over 140 publications and is a member of MRS, AVS, Sigma Xi, and a senior member of the IEEE. Professor Schwarz is a member of the participating research team at the Brookhaven neutron reflectometer, and is co-director of the Garcia Center for Polymers at Engineered Interfaces, an NSF MRSEC. Schwarz is co-director of the Garcia Center for Polymers at Engineered Interfaces. Activities at the Center include an annual symposium, and an annual open house for local high school students and teachers.
