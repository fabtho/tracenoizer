This article discusses the application of automatic differentiation to numerical integration algorithms for ordinary differential equations (ODEs), in particular, the ramifications of the fact that AD is applied not only to the solution of such an algorithm, but to the solution procedure itself. This subtle issue can lead to surprising results when AD tools are applied to variable-stepsize, variable-order ODE integrators.
