PULL HANDLES Franco Clivio Early in 1992, FSB approached Franco Clivio in Zurich to see whether he would accept the gargantuan task of lending a new lease of life to a standard design range from the Seventies, the tubular stable-door handle. It was quite some time before FSB worked out what the man was trying to tell them with his pointed jibe. One of the last students at the legendary Ulm Design College (from 1965 to 1968), Clivio had been taught by Walter Zeischegg to think the Bauhaus way.
Wedding a technical notion to a basic organic form redefined, he has patiently fashioned a bijou range of architectural hardware that can quietly claim to be technically and aesthetically rethinking a classic design range. . fsb - allgood Single-minded as his life has been, it comes as no surprise that Franco Clivio will often query the finality of things.
