Peer Support is just a more structured way of doing so by linking students who have successfully completed a Unit with those embarking on it. Set up in September 1996 with funds from the Sir John Cass's Foundation and later from the University, Peer Support has been establishing a network of channels through which students share with each other their learning skills and understanding of course material. The aim is to add to the quality of the student experience here without taking away the independence of any student to find out how to learn and study in a way that best suits him or her.

The course currently focuses on Token Ring, Ethernet, Wireless configurations for Peer to Peer LANs. The lab experiences allow the students to work in teams as they explore the advantages and disadvantage of each technology. After each team has completed its designated goals they are encouraged to link each one of their LANs together.
Most of the networking technologies require physical connections that must be wired throughout the building for computers to be connected. They are given goals on what their network should do.
