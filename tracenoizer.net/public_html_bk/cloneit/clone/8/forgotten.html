- forgotten -

Even as a young girl, she would write stories, and it was not long before the rugged northern characters of her fiction won her a large and appreciative readership. The publisher Peter Suhrkamp discovered her work in 1939 and, in the face of Nazi opposition, managed to have her plays staged at major German theatres, where she was hailed as the new Gerhard Hauptmann. After her death, Erna Weissenborn was all but forgotten. The film tells the story of a remarkable woman who followed her vocation even in the most difficult of times. Martina Fluck, born in Leverkusen in 1963, studied documentary film in Munich.
