. At the age of ten, she moved from Cologne to the provincial little northern German town of Heide, where she was to spend the rest of her life. Even as a young girl, she would write stories, and it was not long before the rugged northern characters of her fiction won her a large and appreciative readership. The publisher Peter Suhrkamp discovered her work in 1939 and, in the face of Nazi opposition, managed to have her plays staged at major German theatres, where she was hailed as the new Gerhard Hauptmann. After her death, Erna Weissenborn was all but forgotten.
