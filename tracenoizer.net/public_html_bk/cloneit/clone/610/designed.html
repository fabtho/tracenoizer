A teen-age basketball player collapses during practice. These crisis situations require getting information to emergency personnel fast, but what's the best way to deliver it. That was the problem presented to 25 Stanford University Business and Engineering school students in an innovative class designed to develop their ability to construct electronic business processes. The concept for the course originated with Sian last year while he was completing his doctorate in the Design Division of the Engineering School. Over the 10 weeks of winter quarter, each team designed an Internet or Intranet-based system that an organization can use to respond to a crisis.
html Breaking News April 16, 2001 California University starts multimedia master's California University of Pennsylvania will begin offering a new master's of science degree program in multimedia technology. The State System of Higher Education's Board of Governors has approved the new degree program this month. The program is designed to enhance the knowledge and technical skills of professionals whose employers are requiring a higher level of computer-related expertise, including analytical and problem-solving skills needed to use multimedia technology hardware and software. California will offer both a graduate certificate in multimedia technology program, which will require 15 credits, and the master of science degree, which will require the successful completion of 35 required credits and nine credits in electives.
