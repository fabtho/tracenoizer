Originally he was named "Duerrbaechler" according to the name of the hamlet and of the inn of Duerrbach, near Riggisberg, where this longhaired tricoloured farm dog was especially appreciated In 1902, 1904 and 1907 subjects of this breed were exhibited at dog shows. In 1907 a few breeders of the region of Burgdorf decided to promote the pure breeding of this native dog by founding the "Schweizerische Duerrbach-Klub" and fixing the characteristic traits of the breed in a first standard. In 1910, at the invitation of the "Duerrbach-Klub", there were already 107 subjects shown by the farmers at this very special event. From that day onward this dog, renamed "Bernese Mountain Dog" (following the example of other breeds of Swiss Mountain Dogs) became repidly known all over Switzerland and Southern Germany.
