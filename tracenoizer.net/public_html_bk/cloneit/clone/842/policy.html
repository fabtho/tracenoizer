"It is just a micro adjustment to meet local specific conditions," says Song Jie, with the Population Expert Committee of the State Family Planning Commission. According to the new laws approved by provinces and autonomous regions, the right for parents who are both single children to have a second baby is protected. Since the late 1970s, China has adopted the one-child policy to curb its population explosion. The policy has turned China into a low birth rate country and churned out many young single children. The policy does not prohibit a husband and wife who are both single children from bearing a second baby.
