. Many genes are used to specify features unique to each type of cell. Liver cells, for example, express genes for enzymes that detoxify poisons, while pancreas cells express genes for making insulin. To know how cells achieve such specialization, scientists need a way to identify which genes each type of cell expresses. Microarray technology now allows us to look at many genes at once and determine which are expressed in a particular cell type.
