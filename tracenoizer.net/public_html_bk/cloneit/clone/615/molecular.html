Electrochemical gradients for ions are maintained across biological membranes by specialized active transport proteins, ion pumps. In spite of their central role in cell function, the molecular mechanisms by which these proteins transport ions across biological membranes are not known.
Another project is the investigation of cardiac muscle excitation-contraction coupling, the signaling process by which the cardiac action potential triggers muscle contraction. . We are currently studying mechanisms of active electrogenic ion transport and muscle excitation-contraction coupling using biophysical and molecular biological techniques. These experiments utilize patch-clamp techniques with single cardiac myocytes and HeLa cells expressing Na,K-ATPase enzymes that contain point mutations in membrane spanning regions of the protein. Our goal is to determine which amino acid residues of the Na,K-ATPase are responsible for ion coordination and voltage-dependent ion binding reactions and, in doing so, identify the changes in enzyme structure that underlie ion transport reaction steps.
