A number of human pathologies are associated with genetic anomalies affecting chromosome 21. APECED disease (OMIM_240300) is a monogenic autoimmune disorder linked to 21q22. 3, which is due to mutations within a novel gene named AIRE for autoimmune regulator. Isolation of all genes encoded by Chromosome 21, integration of transcriptional and physical maps, and large-scale genomic sequencing are expected to provide a complete gene catalogue and a descriptive view of the chromosomal organization. The chromosome 21 gene catalogue will allow systematic positional cloning and candidate gene approaches for identifying the molecular factors playing a role in HSA21-associated diseases.
Upon entry to the Mutation Data Submission Page, the user will be asked for their name, address and contact details. They may then select the type of mutation they wish to submit to Human Genetics Online i. Each page adopts a standard format to ascertain the disease designation, the identity of the gene involved and the extent and location of the mutation. Users submit their data to Human Genetics electronically and Springer-Verlag will acknowledge receipt of the mutation data and provide a temporary Mutation Accession number for use in all correspondence with Human Genetics. Data will only be accepted for electronic publication if they represent the first report of a given mutation in a given gene.
