Huber's work has always revealed a concern with the origin of the work of art (the creative process) and its final display (in a museum or gallery), just as buying art can be seen as a symbol for making a sacrifice to obtain beauty. 'The Post' is the journey made by the image from its place of origin (the studio) to its destination (the place where it is exhibited). 'The Post' can be seen as a metaphor for the transfer of the content of the image (the message) to the spectator, in which the artist's role is that of a postman.
